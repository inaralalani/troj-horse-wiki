Description

What Inspired Us?


Our project was inspired by the personal experience of one of our group members, who underwent a biopsy to rule out a bacterial infection. After learning more about prosthetic joint infections (PJIs), they were surprised at the low sensitivity of traditional culturing methods used to diagnose biofilm-related infections, specifically those which occur in prosthetic joints.

What Problem Do We Want To Solve?


We aim to combat the inaccurate diagnosis of PJIs and the associated antibiotic overprescription that occurs as a result. Trojan Horse is a simple and sustainable prosthetic joint infection (PJI) diagnosis solution. It utilizes DNABII and PNAG as biofilm-related PJI biomarkers to be used in a rapid test, eliminating the need to directly sample the pathogen.
